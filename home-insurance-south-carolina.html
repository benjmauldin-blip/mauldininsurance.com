What Homeowners Insurance Covers

A standard homeowners policy has several components. We make sure you understand exactly what you have — and what you don't.

🏠 Dwelling Coverage

Covers the structure of your home — walls, roof, floors, built-in appliances — if damaged by fire, wind, hail, lightning, and other covered perils.

📦 Personal Property

Covers your belongings — furniture, electronics, clothing, appliances — if stolen or damaged. We review limits to make sure you're not underinsured.

⚖️ Liability Protection

Covers you if someone is injured on your property or you accidentally damage someone else's property. Includes legal defense costs.

🏨 Additional Living Expenses

Pays for temporary housing and living costs if your home becomes uninhabitable after a covered loss. Critical coverage most people don't think about until they need it.

🌊 Flood Insurance

Standard homeowners policies do NOT cover flooding. If you're near Lake Murray or in a flood zone, a separate flood policy may be essential.

💎 Scheduled Personal Property

High-value items like jewelry, art, or firearms may need separate scheduling to be fully covered. We make sure your valuables aren't left exposed.

South Carolina Home Insurance Considerations

SC homeowners face specific risks that affect coverage decisions. Here's what matters most in the Midlands.

⛈️

Severe Weather Exposure

Lexington County averages multiple significant thunderstorm events per year with hail, high winds, and occasional tornadoes. Many policies now carry separate wind and hail deductibles — know yours before you file a claim.

🌊

Flood Risk Near Lake Murray

Homes near Lake Murray, the Saluda River, and low-lying areas face real flood risk that standard HO policies don't cover. A separate flood insurance policy fills this critical gap.

📈

Rising Rebuild Costs

SC construction costs have risen 25–40% since 2020. If your dwelling coverage hasn't been updated, you may be significantly underinsured. We review your replacement cost estimate at every renewal.

🏚️

Aging Roof Concerns

Carriers pay close attention to roof age in SC. Roofs over 20 years old can trigger surcharges, coverage reductions, or non-renewals. Know your roof's status and how your carrier handles it.
