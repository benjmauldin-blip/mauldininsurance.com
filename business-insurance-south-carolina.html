What is business insurance in South Carolina?

Business insurance in South Carolina is a category of coverage that protects companies from financial loss due to liability claims, property damage, lawsuits, and operational disruptions. Most SC small businesses begin with a Business Owners Policy (BOP), which bundles general liability with commercial property coverage at a lower combined cost than buying each separately. SC also requires workers compensation insurance for businesses with 4 or more employees, with limited exceptions.

Common SC business insurance coverages:

  • General liability insurance
  • Commercial property insurance
  • Business interruption coverage
  • Commercial auto insurance
  • Workers compensation (4+ employees)
  • Cyber liability & professional liability

Business Coverage We Offer

From sole proprietors to growing small businesses, we find the right coverage combination for your specific operation.

⚖️ General Liability

Covers bodily injury and property damage claims made against your business. Often required by clients and landlords. The foundation of any business insurance plan.

🏢 Business Owner's Policy (BOP)

Bundles general liability and commercial property coverage into one convenient policy — typically at a lower cost than buying each separately. Ideal for most small businesses.

🚐 Commercial Auto

Personal auto insurance doesn't cover vehicles used for business purposes. If you or your employees drive for work, commercial auto coverage is essential.

👷 Workers' Compensation

Required in SC for most businesses with four or more employees. Covers medical costs and lost wages for employees injured on the job.

💻 Professional Liability

Also called Errors & Omissions (E&O). Protects you if a client claims your professional advice or services caused them financial harm.

🔒 Cyber Liability

Covers costs related to data breaches, cyberattacks, and privacy violations. Increasingly important for any business that stores customer data.
