What STR Insurance Covers That Your HO Policy Doesn't

Short-term rental activity changes your risk profile in ways a standard policy isn't designed for.

⚖️ Guest Liability

If a guest is injured on your property and sues, your standard HO liability won't respond to a claim arising from a commercial rental activity. STR insurance provides dedicated liability for paying guests.

🏠 Property Damage by Guests

Guest-caused damage — intentional or accidental — is often excluded from standard HO policies. STR coverage extends protection to damage caused by short-term renters.

💰 Loss of Rental Income

If a covered event makes your property unavailable, STR insurance can replace the rental income you lose — something a standard HO policy doesn't provide at all.

🔒 Theft by Guests

Theft by a guest is typically excluded from homeowners policies. Proper STR coverage addresses this gap — protecting your belongings when strangers have access to your home.

Finding the Right STR Coverage for Your SC Property

The right policy depends on how you rent — occasionally, frequently, or year-round.

🏡

Occasional Rental (Under 14 Days/Year)

Some HO policies allow limited short-term rental with an endorsement. If you rent infrequently, a home-sharing endorsement added to your existing policy may be the most cost-effective solution.

📅

Regular Rental (14–180 Days/Year)

If you rent regularly but also occupy the property yourself, a standalone short-term rental policy or a hybrid home/STR policy is usually the right fit. We assess your specific rental pattern.

🏢

Full-Time Rental (180+ Days/Year)

Properties rented year-round as STRs are generally treated like investment properties. A commercial dwelling policy or specialized STR policy provides comprehensive protection for full-time hosts.

🌊

Lake Murray Waterfront Properties

STR hosts on Lake Murray face additional considerations — dock liability, watercraft access, and flood exposure. We make sure all waterfront exposures are properly addressed alongside your rental coverage.
