Types of Life Insurance We Offer

There's no one-size-fits-all life insurance policy. We help you understand your options and find the right fit for your goals and budget.

📅

Term Life Insurance

Most Popular

Provides coverage for a set period — typically 10, 20, or 30 years. The most affordable option for most families. Ideal for replacing income, paying off a mortgage, or covering your children's years at home. Simple, straightforward, and cost-effective.

🏦

Whole Life Insurance

Permanent Coverage

Covers you for your entire life and builds cash value over time. Premiums are fixed and the policy never expires. A good fit for those who want lifelong protection and a savings component they can borrow against.

🔄

Universal Life Insurance

Flexible Premiums

Permanent coverage with adjustable premiums and death benefits. Offers more flexibility than whole life while still building cash value. Good for those whose income or coverage needs may change over time.

🕯️

Final Expense Insurance

Simplified Issue

Smaller whole life policies designed to cover funeral and burial costs. No medical exam required. An affordable way for seniors to protect their families from end-of-life expenses.

What Life Insurance Is Used For

Life insurance does more than cover funeral costs. Here's how South Carolina families use it to protect their financial wellbeing.

💰 Income Replacement

Replace lost income so your family can maintain their standard of living if you pass away unexpectedly.

🏠 Mortgage Protection

Make sure your family can stay in their home by covering the remaining mortgage balance.

🎓 College Funding

Ensure your children's education plans stay on track regardless of what happens to you.

💸 Debt Coverage

Cover outstanding debts — car loans, credit cards, student loans — so they don't become your family's burden.

🕯️ Final Expenses

Funeral and burial costs can easily reach $10,000–$15,000. Life insurance ensures this doesn't fall on your loved ones.

🏢 Business Continuation

Business owners use life insurance to fund buy-sell agreements and keep operations running after a partner's death.
