Auto Coverage Options We Shop

Every driver's situation is different. We find the right combination of coverage for your vehicle, your budget, and South Carolina's requirements.

🛡️ Liability Coverage

Required by South Carolina law. Covers bodily injury and property damage you cause to others in an accident. We make sure you have adequate limits — not just the minimum.

💥 Collision Coverage

Pays to repair or replace your vehicle if it's damaged in a collision, regardless of fault. Important if you have a newer or financed vehicle.

🌪️ Comprehensive Coverage

Covers non-collision damage — theft, hail, flooding, deer strikes, and more. South Carolina weather makes this coverage worth having.

🚑 Medical Payments

Covers medical expenses for you and your passengers after an accident, regardless of who was at fault.

⚠️ Uninsured Motorist

Protects you when the other driver has no insurance or not enough. South Carolina has a significant uninsured driver population — this coverage matters.

🔧 Roadside Assistance

Towing, flat tire, battery jump, and lockout services. Often available as an affordable add-on that can save you hundreds when you need it.

Ways to Save on Auto Insurance in SC

There are more ways to lower your premium than most people realize. Here are some we look for on your behalf.

1

Bundle Auto + Home

Combining your auto and homeowners policies with one carrier often saves 10–25% on both premiums.

2

Raise Your Deductible

Increasing your deductible from $500 to $1,000 can meaningfully lower your monthly premium if you're a safe driver.

3

Good Driver Discounts

Clean driving records are rewarded. We find carriers that offer the biggest discounts for your specific history.

4

Multi-Vehicle Discounts

Insuring more than one vehicle on the same policy typically results in a per-vehicle discount.

5

Shop at Renewal

Rates change every year. We review your policy at renewal and re-shop if a better rate is available.

6

Pay-in-Full Discount

Many carriers offer a discount if you pay your 6-month or annual premium in full rather than monthly.
