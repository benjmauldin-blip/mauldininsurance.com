The Cheapest Bundle Trap

A common mistake: buying the lowest auto rate from one carrier and the lowest home rate from another. Sounds smart, but it almost always costs more than a properly-shopped bundle.

  • The math problem: Carrier A may have the cheapest auto in your ZIP code, while Carrier B has the cheapest home. Without the multi-policy discount, your combined cost is often higher than picking the best bundle from a single carrier.
  • The hidden cost: Two carriers means two billing cycles, two renewals to track, two claims processes if something goes wrong, two policies that might not coordinate after a major loss.
  • The right approach: Compare bundled quotes across 5+ carriers with their stacking discounts applied. Then compare against the standalone-cheapest-each option. Whichever is cheaper wins. Most of the time, the bundle wins.
Most SC homeowners haven't shopped their bundle in 3+ years. Carrier rates have changed dramatically since 2022. The carrier that was the cheapest bundle in 2022 may not be the cheapest in 2026 — and vice versa. A 30-minute call with us can confirm whether you're overpaying.
