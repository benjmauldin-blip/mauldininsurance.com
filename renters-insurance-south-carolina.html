What Renters Insurance Covers

For roughly the cost of a couple of meals out per month, renters insurance gives you three important types of protection.

📦 Personal Property

Covers your belongings — electronics, furniture, clothing, kitchen items — if they're stolen, destroyed by fire, or damaged by certain events. Covers items both at home and away from home in many cases.

⚖️ Personal Liability

If someone is injured in your apartment and sues you, or if you accidentally damage someone else's property, liability coverage helps pay for legal costs and settlements.

🏨 Loss of Use

If your rental becomes uninhabitable due to a covered loss — like a fire — this coverage helps pay for temporary housing and extra living expenses while repairs are made.

💊 Medical Payments

Covers minor medical expenses if a guest is injured in your home, regardless of fault. Helps you handle situations without going through a formal liability claim.

Common Renters Insurance Myths

Most people who don't have renters insurance have been misled by one of these common misconceptions.

❌

"My landlord's insurance covers my stuff."

Your landlord's policy covers the building structure only. Your personal belongings, your liability, and your living expenses if displaced have zero coverage under their policy.

❌

"I don't have enough stuff to make it worth it."

Most people underestimate what they own. Add up your electronics, clothing, furniture, and kitchen items — it's likely worth $15,000 to $30,000 or more.

❌

"Renters insurance is expensive."

Renters insurance is often the most affordable policy available — frequently $15–$25 per month for solid coverage. It's one of the best values in all of insurance.

❌

"I only need it if my landlord requires it."

Whether required or not, renters insurance protects YOU. A fire, theft, or guest injury without coverage means those losses come entirely out of your pocket.
