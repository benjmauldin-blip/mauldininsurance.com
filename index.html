Property & Casualty Insurance in South Carolina — A Plain-English Guide

Mauldin Insurance Group is an independent insurance agency based in Lexington, South Carolina, serving individuals, families, and business owners across all of South Carolina, North Carolina, and Georgia. Founded in 2021 by Ben and Jennifer Mauldin, the agency is licensed across all three states and represents multiple top-rated insurance carriers, allowing it to compare coverage and pricing rather than sell a single company's products.

Property and casualty (P&C) insurance is a category of coverage that protects you from financial loss in two ways: property insurance covers damage to your physical assets — your home, vehicle, business property, or boat — and casualty insurance protects you from liability if you are legally responsible for injury or damage to another person. Together, P&C insurance forms the foundation of personal and business financial protection in South Carolina.

This guide covers the eight core P&C insurance lines available in South Carolina — auto, homeowners, renters, landlord, business, general liability, commercial auto, and boat insurance — along with how each line works in the SC market, what the state requires, and how an independent agent compares carriers to find the right combination of coverage and price.

Personal & Business Insurance Coverage in South Carolina

Definitional summaries of the most important property and casualty lines we write — with the SC-specific context, requirements, and risks that matter when you choose a policy.

1. Auto Insurance in South Carolina

Auto insurance protects you financially in the event of an accident, theft, or damage involving your vehicle. South Carolina requires minimum liability coverage of 25/50/25 — $25,000 bodily injury per person, $50,000 per accident, and $25,000 property damage — plus uninsured motorist coverage at the same limits. Most drivers carry higher limits because at-fault accident costs frequently exceed minimum coverage.

  • Liability coverage (required in SC)
  • Collision coverage (covers your vehicle in an accident)
  • Comprehensive coverage (theft, hail, fallen tree)
  • Uninsured / underinsured motorist (required in SC)
  • Medical payments & PIP coverage
  • Rental car & roadside assistance
Learn more about auto insurance in SC →

2. Homeowners Insurance in South Carolina

Homeowners insurance protects your home, belongings, and personal liability from covered losses such as fire, wind, hail, theft, and lawsuits. SC home insurance premiums have risen 25 to 40 percent since 2022 due to rising rebuild costs and weather-related claim frequency. Two important exclusions to know: standard policies do not cover flood damage or earthquake damage in South Carolina — both require separate policies.

  • Dwelling protection (the structure)
  • Other structures (detached garage, fence, shed)
  • Personal property coverage
  • Loss of use (temporary living expenses)
  • Personal liability protection
  • Medical payments to guests
Learn more about home insurance in SC →

3. Renters Insurance in South Carolina

Renters insurance protects tenants from financial loss due to theft, fire, water damage, or liability while renting an apartment, house, or condo. Your landlord's policy covers the building structure but does not cover your belongings or your liability. Renters policies in SC typically cost between $12 and $25 per month for $20,000 to $40,000 of coverage — among the most affordable forms of insurance available.

  • Personal property protection
  • Personal liability coverage
  • Additional living expenses
  • Medical payments to guests
  • Loss of use after a covered claim
  • Theft coverage on or off premises
Learn more about renters insurance in SC →

4. Landlord Insurance in South Carolina

Landlord insurance — also called a dwelling fire policy or DP-3 — covers non-owner-occupied rental properties owned by South Carolina property investors. Standard homeowners policies are written for owner-occupied properties and may be voided the moment you rent your home to a tenant. A landlord policy specifically covers the dwelling, liability for tenant injuries, and loss of rental income during covered claims.

  • Dwelling property coverage
  • Liability for tenant or visitor injuries
  • Loss of rental income (fair rental value)
  • Other structures on the rental property
  • Optional vandalism and theft coverage
  • Medical payments coverage
Learn more about landlord insurance in SC →

5. Business & Commercial Insurance in South Carolina

Business insurance protects South Carolina companies from financial loss due to liability claims, property damage, lawsuits, and operational disruptions. Most SC small businesses start with a Business Owners Policy (BOP), which bundles general liability with commercial property coverage at a lower combined cost than buying each separately. Industries with higher exposure — contractors, restaurants, professional services — often need additional specialty coverages.

  • General liability insurance
  • Commercial property insurance
  • Business interruption coverage
  • Commercial auto insurance
  • Workers compensation (required in SC for 4+ employees)
  • Cyber liability & professional liability
Learn more about business insurance in SC →

6. General Liability Insurance for South Carolina Businesses

General liability insurance protects South Carolina businesses from third-party claims involving bodily injury, property damage, and personal injury such as libel, slander, or copyright infringement. It also covers legal defense costs even when claims are unfounded. General liability is one of the most essential coverages for any SC business and is typically a requirement for commercial leases, contractor licenses, and many vendor contracts.

  • Bodily injury claims
  • Property damage claims
  • Personal & advertising injury
  • Legal defense costs
  • Medical payments to non-employees
  • Products & completed operations coverage
Learn more about general liability in SC →

7. Commercial Auto Insurance in South Carolina

Commercial auto insurance covers vehicles used for business purposes in South Carolina, including delivery vehicles, service trucks, contractor fleets, and any vehicle titled in a business name. Personal auto policies typically exclude business use beyond commuting — meaning a denied claim if you're driving on the job. SC commercial auto follows the same 25/50/25 minimums as personal auto, but most fleets carry significantly higher limits.

  • Vehicles titled in a business name
  • Service trucks & contractor vehicles
  • Delivery vehicles & food service
  • Hired and non-owned auto coverage
  • Higher liability limits than personal auto
  • Cargo & equipment coverage available
Learn more about commercial auto in SC →

8. Boat & Watercraft Insurance — Lake Murray, SC

Boat insurance protects watercraft against physical damage, liability for accidents, and theft. South Carolina does not legally require boat insurance, but most Lake Murray marinas require liability coverage for slip rental — and Lake Murray is one of the most heavily trafficked recreational lakes in the Southeast on summer weekends. Modern boat replacement costs frequently exceed $30,000, and an at-fault accident without coverage can result in personal liability for tens of thousands of dollars.

  • Physical damage coverage (collision, sinking, fire)
  • Liability protection (bodily injury & property)
  • Theft & vandalism coverage
  • Fuel spill liability (federal requirement)
  • Trailer coverage available
  • Personal effects on the boat
