What Boat Insurance Covers

A standard boat policy provides comprehensive protection on and off the water.

⛵ Physical Damage

Covers your vessel, motor, and equipment against collision, fire, theft, storm damage, sinking, and vandalism. Choose between Agreed Value (no depreciation) or Actual Cash Value.

⚖️ Liability Coverage

Covers you if your boat causes bodily injury or property damage to another person. Pays medical expenses, property damage, and legal defense costs if you're sued.

🚑 Medical Payments

Pays medical expenses for you, your family, and your passengers after a boating accident — regardless of fault. Important since health insurance coverage for boating injuries varies.

⚠️ Uninsured Boater

If an uninsured boater hits you and causes injuries or damage, this coverage pays when their insurance is insufficient — critical since SC doesn't require boat insurance.

🔧 On-Water Towing

Pays for emergency towing if you break down on the water. A Lake Murray tow to a marina can run $300–$800 without this coverage.

🏠 Dock & Lift Coverage

Your dock, boat lift, and covered boathouse may need endorsements or separate scheduling. We make sure your waterfront structures are adequately covered.

Lake Murray Coverage Considerations

Lake Murray has specific characteristics that affect your boat insurance needs.

🌊

High Traffic on Summer Weekends

Lake Murray is one of the most heavily trafficked recreational lakes in the Southeast on summer weekends. Dense boat traffic increases accident probability — adequate liability coverage is especially important here.

🏄

Jet Ski and PWC Coverage

Personal watercraft are among the most common sources of boating injury claims. Some boat policies extend to cover PWC — others require a separate watercraft policy. Confirm both are covered.

🎣

Pontoon and Family Boats

Pontoon boats are SC's most popular family watercraft. They frequently carry multiple passengers, making medical payments coverage especially important. Pontoon policies typically run $400–$900/year.

🏡

Dock and Waterfront Structures

A private dock worth $50,000+, a lift worth $15,000, and a covered structure — these can easily exceed your homeowners "other structures" budget. Verify they're explicitly covered.

Boat Insurance FAQs

SC does not legally require boat insurance. However, most marinas require proof of liability insurance before renting or maintaining a slip. If your boat is financed, your lender almost certainly requires physical damage coverage. And operating without liability coverage means you're personally responsible for the full cost of any accident you cause.
Rates vary by vessel type, size, engine, age, value, and coverage levels. General SC ranges: small fishing/bass boat under $20,000 value: $150–$350/year; pontoon boat $40,000–$80,000 value: $400–$900/year; jet ski/PWC: $200–$500/year; ski boat $30,000–$60,000: $350–$750/year. Bundling with your home and auto often yields additional discounts.
Homeowners policies typically cover boats with very limited liability and property coverage — often capping at $1,500 for the vessel. For any boat with meaningful value or for use on a lake as active as Lake Murray, a standalone boat policy is essential for proper protection.
Your homeowners "other structures" coverage may include some dock coverage but typically has sub-limits that are inadequate for high-value waterfront structures. Docks, boat lifts, and covered boathouses on Lake Murray can easily represent $65,000–$150,000 in assets that need to be explicitly covered — either through endorsements, scheduled property, or a separate policy.
