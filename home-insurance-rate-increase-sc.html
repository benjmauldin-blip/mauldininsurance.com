Why SC Home Insurance Rates Are Rising

Understanding why rates went up helps you fight back more effectively.

🏗️ Construction Cost Surge

SC rebuild costs rose 25–40% since 2020. Carriers are raising rates to match higher replacement costs — and many homeowners are still underinsured relative to actual rebuild value.

⛈️ Increased Storm Frequency

More frequent severe storms, hail events, and wind damage across the Midlands are driving up claims — which pushes rates up across the board, even for homeowners with no claims.

📉 Carrier Pullbacks

Several national carriers have reduced their SC presence or tightened underwriting. Less competition in some markets means higher rates — which is exactly why having an independent agent matters.

🏚️ Roof Age Concerns

Many Lexington-area homes built in the 1990s now have aging roofs. Carriers are pricing roof age risk aggressively — and in some cases non-renewing policies with older roofs.

How to Push Back on Your Rate Increase

There are real levers you can pull — beyond just accepting what your current carrier says.

🔍

Shop the Market

Your current carrier has no obligation to offer you the best rate. We compare multiple top-rated carriers side by side — often finding savings of $300–$700/year for the same or better coverage.

📦

Bundle Home and Auto

Multi-policy discounts of 10–25% are common when you move both home and auto to the same carrier. We look at bundled and unbundled options to find what actually saves you the most.

🔧

Update Your Coverage Levels

Paradoxically, some homeowners are overinsured on contents and underinsured on dwelling. A coverage review often finds ways to realign limits and reduce premium without increasing risk.

🏚️

Address the Roof

A new roof can dramatically reduce your premium — often paying for itself within a few renewal cycles. We help you understand the math before you commit to a replacement.
